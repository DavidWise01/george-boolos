GEORGE BOOLOS (1940–1996) — LOGIKĒ, the modern foundations; the CLARIFIER. [[kurt-godel]] proved incompleteness with heavy machinery; Boolos showed it all snaps into a tiny MODAL LOGIC — read the box □ not as 'necessarily' ([[saul-kripke]]) but as 'IT IS PROVABLE THAT', and provability in arithmetic is governed by one short system, ⚑ GL, whose distinctive law is LÖB'S THEOREM □(□p→p)→□p. Incompleteness becomes a one-line modal fact: the system can't prove its own consistency, ¬□⊥ (Gödel's SECOND theorem). LIVE: the two self-referential sentences — Gödel's G↔¬□G ('I am NOT provable') is TRUE BUT UNPROVABLE, while Henkin/Löb's H↔□H ('I AM provable') is actually PROVABLE (Löb's theorem) — click each to follow its logic; the asymmetry IS incompleteness (verified). CONFIDENCE-TIERED: STRONG (provability logic GL is a precise, complete modal account of provability — Solovay; 'The Logic of Provability' 1993 is the standard text; re-derives Gödel & Löb in clean modal lines, tied to [[saul-kripke]]'s semantics) / A-REFRAMING-NOT-A-NEW-THEOREM (the core results are Gödel's & Löb's & the completeness is Solovay's; Boolos's gift was the unifying modal VIEW & unmatched exposition) / FAMOUS-FOR-PUZZLES-TOO (⚠ the 'Hardest Logic Puzzle Ever' (three gods) refines Smullyan; Gödel's 2nd in words of ONE SYLLABLE — dazzling exposition, not his deepest math). A great teacher of logic, co-author of 'Computability and Logic', a crossword champion; died of cancer at 55. ⚑ He took the hardest theorem in this lineage and handed it back as a small clean modal logic.
